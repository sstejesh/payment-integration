ABOUT ORPHANS

An orphan is a child whose parents have died or unknown or have permanently abandoned them.
In common usage, only a child who has lost both parents due to death is called an orphan.
Orphans are relatively rare in developed countries, because most children can expect both of their parents to survive their childhood.
In the common use, an orphan does not have any surviving parent to care for them.
Much higher numbers of orphans exist in war-torn nations such as Afghanistan.
Wars and great epidemics such as AIDS, have created many orphans.
